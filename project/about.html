Process

The Steps I Take to Make Decisions

Making an investment descision is a complicated process. You need to collect a lot of information to make a good decision. This is a list of data points I gather and how I evaluate them.

Business Description

In simple terms, I need to be able to describe the business. What does it do, and how does it make money?

Industry Analysis & Competitive Positioning

No Business operates in a vacuum. What are the forces that drive the industry, and how do they affect the company's ability to compete?

Macroeconomic Environment

How does the overall economy look? How might that affect the business? How would changes in monetary policy affect the company?

Investment Risks and Returns

Next, I look at historical returns on the investmemt over the past few years. How does that compare to the market? How much variance is there month-to-month?

Portfolio Analysis

Based on historical returns, determine if the asset would fit well within an optimal portfolio

Financial Analysis

This is the meat and potatoes of stock analysis. Analyze the financial statements and determine a valuation The following are excellent tools to use.

  • Income generation (dividends)
  • Financial statement analysis (ratios)
  • Relative valuation
  • P/E
  • Discounted cash flow
  • Free cash flow model
  • Comparative firm(s) analysis

Environmental, Social, Governance

Is the company meeting investor expectations in these areas?

Behavioral Finance

Acknoledge your own biases. Do you like/dislike to company personally? How might that affect your decision making?

Decision

State your decision. Will you buy, sell, or hold the stock?
